🎛️ Vacuum Tube Controls

🔥

Heat Generation

Vacuum tubes generated enormous heat, requiring massive cooling systems. A single ENIAC computer used 17,468 vacuum tubes and consumed 150 kW of power!

⚡

How It Works

Electrons flow from the heated cathode to the anode through a vacuum. The grid controls this flow, acting like a valve for electricity.

💡

Applications

Used in early computers, radios, and TVs. The first electronic computer, ENIAC (1946), weighed 30 tons and occupied 1,800 square feet!
